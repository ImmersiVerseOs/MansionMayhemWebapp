### üöÄ Patent Filing Options

#### Option A: DIY Provisional Patents (Lowest Cost)

**Cost:** ~$300-$400 total
- $150 USPTO filing fee (small entity) per patent √ó 2 = $300
- Your time to finalize and file

**Timeline:**
- Finalize documents: 1-2 days
- File online at USPTO.gov: Same day
- Receive confirmation: 1-2 weeks

**Benefits:**
- Immediate "Patent Pending" status
- 12-month priority date
- Can refine before full patent

**Steps:**
1. Review both provisional patent documents
2. Create simple diagrams (can be hand-drawn and scanned)
3. Register at USPTO.gov (https://www.uspto.gov/patents/basics/patent-process)
4. File using EFS-Web system
5. Pay $150 per application (small entity rate)

#### Option B: Patent Attorney (Recommended)

**Cost:** ~$4,000-$10,000 total
- $2,000-$5,000 per provisional patent √ó 2

**Timeline:**
- Attorney review and refinement: 2-3 weeks
- File with USPTO: Same day after finalization
- Receive confirmation: 1-2 weeks

**Benefits:**
- Professional review of claims
- Stronger legal foundation
- Attorney guides full patent process
- Better chance of approval

**Steps:**
1. Find patent attorney specializing in software/AI (Michigan Bar preferred)
2. Share provisional patent documents
3. Attorney refines claims and creates professional diagrams
4. Attorney files on your behalf

**Finding an Attorney:**
- Michigan State Bar referral: https://www.michbar.org/
- USPTO Attorney Search: https://oedci.uspto.gov/OEDCI/
- Look for: Software patents, AI/ML experience, provisional patent experience

#### Option C: Delay Patent Filing (Not Recommended)

**Risk:** Lose "first to file" priority if competitor files similar patent

**Only delay if:**
- You're not showing the technology publicly yet
- You have very limited funds
- You plan to file within 6 months
